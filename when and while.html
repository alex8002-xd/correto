When and While


When is used to indicate that an action occurs at a specific time or events that occur at the same time in the past, present or future.


Ejemplos:


To refer to a specific moment in time:
I was eating when the phone rang.
(Estaba comiendo cuando sonó el teléfono.)



To talk about situations that happen simultaneously or at the same moment:
She was happy when she heard the news.
(Ella estaba feliz cuando escuchó la noticia.)


To refer to situations in which one action happens after another:
I´ll call you when i arrive.
(Te llamaré cuando llegue.)

While


While is used to talk about simultaneous actions or situations in a period of time. The difference The main thing with "when" is that "while" implies that two actions are happening at the same time.

Ejemplos:


To describe two actions that occur simultaneously:
She was reading while i was cooking.
To talk about a continuous action over a period of time:
I like to listen to music while studying.
While:It is used to contrast two situations.
He likes tea, while i prefer coffee.
